    Methodology

    This dashboard is a planning and screening tool, not a legal eviction prediction model. It combines household-entered budget information with local county economic indicators from ACS 5-year county data.

    The household component uses housing burden, monthly budget cushion, and emergency savings coverage. Housing burden equals your monthly rent plus your monthly utilities divided by your monthly income. Monthly cushion equals your income minus rent, utilities, other essential expenses, and required debt payments. Savings coverage equals emergency savings divided by monthly essential expenses.

    The county component uses poverty rate, unemployment rate, median gross rent relative to county median household income, renter share, and severe renter cost burden. These indicators are converted to bounded 0–100 pressure scores and combined into a local economic stress score. The final planning score gives greater weight to your own household budget because immediate household finances are the closest input to planning needs.

    Data note: The dashboard automatically uses the latest ACS 5-year county record available in data/county_indicators.json. In most current use, this will be the 2020–2024 ACS 5-year estimate. The dashboard does not ask users to choose a year because the data are not live 2026 observations.

    Important limitation: This tool does not predict eviction, provide legal advice, or determine eligibility for assistance. Housing outcomes also depend on lease terms, landlord decisions, local laws, court processes, emergency aid availability, and household circumstances not entered here.
